Insights constantly tracks the performance of all the projects of your <NT><GlossTerm baseform="Workspace">Workspace</GlossTerm></NT>s. You can get aggregated build data of all your projects, detailed metrics of each project, and you can even categorize the metrics as you see fit using dashboards. Insights also allows you to view bottlenecks: it shows you the <NT><GlossTerm baseform="workflow">Workflow</GlossTerm></NT>s and tests that take the most time or cost you the highest amount of credits.

To get to your <NT>Workspace</NT>'s <NT>Insights</NT> page:

1. <NT>Log in</NT> to your <NT>Bitrise</NT> account.
1. In the top right corner, open the account selector dropdown menu and select a <NT>Workspace</NT>.
1. On the header, click **<NT>Insights</NT>**. This takes you to the **<NT>Overview</NT>** page of the selected <NT>Workspace</NT>.

## The <NT>Overview</NT> page

On the **<NT>Overview</NT>**, you can see aggregated build data of the selected <NT>Workspace</NT>. You can see both aggregated build data and project-level build data in the form of charts that cover the [selected timeframe](/en/insights/getting-started-with-insights#viewing-data-from-a-specific-timeframe).

![Insights_overview.gif](/img/_paligo/uuid-dbb181a9-053e-bed3-133f-11e485313efa.gif)

You can switch between <NT>Workspaces</NT> at any time: in the top right corner, open the account selector dropdown menu and select a <NT>Workspace</NT>.

## Accessing the build metrics

You can get to the detailed build metrics of your <NT>Workspace</NT> or a specific app in two ways: you can simply select **<NT>Builds</NT>** on the left, or you can choose a specific metric from the **<NT>Overview</NT>** page of the <NT>Workspace</NT>. We'll go through the second method:

1. Open the **<NT>Overview</NT>** page of your <NT>Workspace</NT>.
1. Find the metric you need. For example, **<NT>Build failure rate</NT>**.
1. Click **<NT>View details</NT>**.

   If you have more than one project in the <NT>Workspace</NT>, on the <NT>Details</NT> page you will see the aggregated metrics for all projects of the <NT>Workspace</NT> by default.
1. From the leftmost dropdown menu, select the project you need.
1. Optionally, you can dig deeper by adding filters using the **<NT>Add filter</NT>** button to view specific data. You can filter for:

   - <NT><GlossTerm baseform="pipeline">Pipeline</GlossTerm></NT>s: metrics of builds that ran with a specific <NT>Pipeline</NT> of the project.
   - Stages: metric of builds that ran with a specific Stage of a <NT>Pipeline</NT>.
   - <NT>Workflows</NT>: metrics of builds that ran with a specific <NT>Workflow</NT> of the project.
   - Branches: metrics of builds of a specific branch.
   - <NT>Machine types</NT>: metrics of builds that ran on a specific machine type.
   - <NT><GlossTerm baseform="stack">Stack</GlossTerm></NT>s: metrics of builds that ran on a specific stack.

   ![filtering_highlight.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-0242fdf7-4025-4aca-215a-ffbecf5e62b9.png)
1. Scroll down to see individual builds, their data, including the <NT><GlossTerm baseform="step">Step</GlossTerm></NT>s they ran with.

For the available build metrics, check out [Bitrise CI metrics](/en/insights/available-metrics-in-insights/bitrise-ci-metrics).

## Accessing the testing metrics of an app

Insights allows you to keep track of testing metrics, right down to the level of individual testing modules. To do this, you need a testing <NT>Step</NT> that exports its results to the [**Deploy to Bitrise.io - Build Artifacts, Test Reports, and Pipeline intermediate files**](https://github.com/bitrise-steplib/steps-deploy-to-bitrise-io) <NT>Step</NT>. The following <NT>Steps</NT> do this automatically, without any need for additional configuration:

- [**Xcode Test for iOS**](https://github.com/bitrise-steplib/steps-xcode-test)
- [**Android Unit Test**](https://github.com/bitrise-steplib/bitrise-step-android-unit-test)
- [**iOS Device Testing**](https://github.com/bitrise-steplib/steps-virtual-device-testing-for-ios)
- [**Virtual Device Testing for Android**](https://github.com/bitrise-steplib/steps-virtual-device-testing-for-android)
- [**Flutter Test**](https://github.com/bitrise-steplib/bitrise-step-flutter-test)

If you use any of these <NT>Steps</NT> to run your tests, you can check your metrics in Insights:

1. Open Insights.
1. On the left, select **<NT>Tests</NT>**.
1. From the leftmost dropdown menu, select the project you need.
1. Optionally, you can dig deeper by using filters to view specific testing data. You can filter for:

   - <NT>Test suite</NT>s
   - <NT>Test cases</NT>
   - Modules
   - Branches

   ![testing_metrics.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-ce8ef015-6c7d-9b31-d3ce-69669a18b4ac.png)
1. Scroll down to see the individual test cases.

For the available test metrics, check out [Bitrise CI metrics](/en/insights/available-metrics-in-insights/bitrise-ci-metrics).

## Bottlenecks

The **<NT>Bottlenecks</NT>** section helps you understand where you can save the most time or credits. We show you which of your <NT>Workflows</NT> and tests take the longest time and cost the highest amount of credits.

To access the <NT>Bottlenecks</NT> page, simply select **<NT>Bottlenecks</NT>** on the left. The bottlenecks are divided into the three basic categories we use for all metrics: **<NT>Builds</NT>**, **<NT>Tests</NT>**, and **<NT>Credits</NT>**. Each category has three metrics:

- **<NT>Failing Workflows</NT>/Failing tests**: these are the <NT>failing Workflows</NT> or test cases that have the highest impact on the time or credits used on your builds.
- **<NT>Build time</NT>/<NT>Run time</NT>**: <NT>Workflows</NT> with increasing build times, or test cases with increasing run times.
- **<NT>Usage</NT>/<NT>Flakiness</NT>**: <NT>Workflows</NT> that consumed the highest amount of resources (either time or credits), or test cases with the most flaky runs.


## Viewing data from a specific timeframe

By default, all Insights pages display your metrics on a weekly basis from the last 12 weeks. Modify the basis and timeframe by opening the respective dropdown menu in the top right of the page, and selecting the options you need.

## The <NT>Dashboards</NT> page

You can use the Insights <NT>Dashboards</NT> page to categorize the charts of your <NT>Workspace</NT> so that you can view them in one place.

You can combine charts from the Builds, Tests, and <NT>Credits</NT> tab into a single dashboard, and you can have as many dashboards as you want.

![dashboards.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-fc575279-c87c-1046-a747-4ea6a59fae5f.png)

### Creating a new dashboard

You can create a new dashboard either from the <NT>Dashboards</NT> page or by clicking on the **<NT>Add to dashboard</NT>** button (![Add_To_dashboard.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-26a387b3-ea77-211f-b2f8-09325e1cc44a.png)) and clicking the **<NT>Create dashboard</NT>** button. We will focus on the former method in this guide:

1. Open Insights.
1. On the left, select **<NT>Dashboards</NT>**.
1. Click on **<NT>Create dashboard</NT>** to open the **<NT>Create dashboard</NT>** window.

   ![Create_dashboard.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-6da7408c-facc-9a1a-b494-b9acdf47accb.png)
1. Add the name of the dashboard under the **<NT>Dashboard name</NT>** filed and optionally add a description under the **<NT>Description</NT>** field.
1. Select the <NT>default view</NT> of granularity and <NT>time range</NT>. You can also change these options later.
1. Click on **<NT>Create</NT>**.

### Accessing a dashboard

To access a dashboard:

1. Open Insights.
1. On the left, select **<NT>Dashboards</NT>**.
1. Click on the name of the dashboard or the arrow icon ("![project_selector_arrow.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-06b2413b-0794-39ff-c8b0-176c85c8e1e1.png)") to open a specific dashboard.

From here, you can see the charts you previously added to your dashboard. For more information, check out [Adding a chart to a dashboard](/en/insights/getting-started-with-insights#adding-a-chart-to-a-dashboard).

### Adding a chart to a dashboard

You can add any chart from the **<NT>Builds</NT>**, **<NT>Tests</NT>**, and **<NT>Credits</NT>** tabs to your dashboard:

1. Open Insights.
1. Select the **<NT>Builds</NT>**, **<NT>Tests</NT>**, or **<NT>Credits</NT>** tab on the left.
1. Search for the metric you want to add to your dashboard.
1. Click on the **<NT>Add to dashboard</NT>** ( ![Add_To_dashboard.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-26a387b3-ea77-211f-b2f8-09325e1cc44a.png) ) button next to it.
1. Insert a name under the **<NT>Chart name</NT>** field.
1. Select the dashboard where you want to place this chart using the dropdown menu.
1. Click **<NT>Add</NT>**.

That's it! From now on you will be able to see your chart on the **<NT>Dashboard</NT>** page.

:::tip[Removing a chart from a dashboard]

You can remove a chart from a dashboard using the edit mode. For more information, check out [Editing a dashboard](/en/insights/getting-started-with-insights#editing-a-dashboard).

:::

### Editing a dashboard

Once you have created a dashboard, you can edit it as you see fit. Among other things, you can edit the default <NT>time range</NT> or delete charts you may no longer need. To do so, you must use the **<NT>Edit mode</NT>**:

1. Open Insights.
1. On the left, select **<NT>Dashboards</NT>**.
1. [Access the dashboard](/en/insights/getting-started-with-insights#accessing-a-dashboard) you would like to edit.
1. Click the options menu (⋮), then click **<NT>Edit mode</NT>**.

   ![Edit_mode.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-d21f2840-9772-4870-9818-a3fd1162be1f.png)
1. In the edit mode, you may change the dashboard's <NT>general settings</NT> by clicking on the **<NT>Settings</NT>** button. You can also change the name of previously added charts or remove them using the icons next to them.

   ![Edit_mode_opened.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-cac01df1-7cc0-598f-62b5-87a237ecc669.png)
1. Click on **<NT>Save</NT>** after you finish editing the dashboard.


## Creating alerts

You can create alerts for metrics that you would like to keep an eye on to <NT><GlossTerm baseform="trigger">trigger</GlossTerm></NT> when a specific threshold is reached. You can then push the alert notifications to <NT>Slack</NT> channels, email addresses, or Teams channels.

To learn more about alerts: [Configuring alerts in Insights](/en/insights/configuring-alerts-in-insights).
