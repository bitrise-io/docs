You can configure your <NT><GlossTerm baseform="Workspace">workspace</GlossTerm></NT> to only allow official <NT>Bitrise</NT> <NT><GlossTerm baseform="Step">Step</GlossTerm></NT>s in its projects.

By default, you can place any <NT>Step</NT> in your <NT><GlossTerm baseform="Workflow">Workflow</GlossTerm></NT>s, including community <NT>Steps</NT> and custom <NT>Steps</NT>. However, you can disable third-party <NT>Steps</NT>, including:

- [Verified Steps](/en/bitrise-ci/workflows-and-pipelines/developing-your-own-bitrise-step/verified-steps)
- Community <NT>Steps</NT>

## Third-party <NT>Step</NT> restrictions

If you disable third-party <NT>Steps</NT>:

- If your <NT><GlossTerm baseform="Configuration YAML">configuration YAML</GlossTerm></NT> is [stored on bitrise.io](/en/bitrise-ci/configure-builds/configuration-yaml/storing-an-apps-configuration-yaml#storing-the-bitriseyml-file-in-your-repository), builds with third‑party <NT>Steps</NT> will start, but you can't save <NT>Workflow</NT> changes until the <NT>Steps</NT> are removed.
- If your configuration YAML is [stored in the repository](/en/bitrise-ci/configure-builds/configuration-yaml/storing-an-apps-configuration-yaml#storing-the-bitriseyml-file-in-your-repository), we cannot validate it and builds won’t start.
- <NT>Script</NT> <NT>Steps</NT> are still present and can run arbitrary code, including [third-party tooling](/en/bitrise-ci/configure-builds/configuring-build-settings/configuring-tool-versions).
- When using the <NT><GlossTerm baseform="Workflow Editor">Workflow Editor</GlossTerm></NT> on <NT>Bitrise</NT>, you won't be able to select third-party <NT>Steps</NT>, only <NT>Steps</NT> maintained by <NT>Bitrise</NT>.


## Disabling third-party <NT>Steps</NT>

To disable third-party <NT>Steps</NT>:

1. <Partial_OpeningTheWorkspaceSettingsPage />
1. Select **<NT>Security</NT>**.
1. Select the **<NT>Build policies</NT>** tab.
1. Optionally, request a report of <NT>Workflows</NT> using third-party <NT>Steps</NT> by clicking **<NT>Request report</NT>**.
1. Toggle **<NT>Allow third-party Steps</NT>** to off.


## Requesting an email report of third-party <NT>Steps</NT>

You can request a usage report of third-party <NT>Steps</NT>: it contains all occurrences of third-party <NT>Steps</NT> in all <NT>Workflows</NT>, <NT>Step bundles</NT>, and <NT>Pipelines</NT> in a CSV file, sent via email.

To request a usage report:

1. Open the **<NT>Workspace settings</NT>** page.
1. Select **<NT>Security</NT>**.
1. Select the **<NT>Build policies</NT>** tab.
1. Request a report of <NT>Workflows</NT> using third-party <NT>Steps</NT> by clicking **<NT>Request report</NT>**.
