## About <NT>Verified Steps</NT>

A <NT>Step</NT> contains the code that performs a specific build task. <NT>Bitrise</NT> has over 400 <NT>Steps</NT> in its [Step Library (StepLib)](https://github.com/bitrise-io/bitrise-steplib) which third party companies or open source teams can enrich with <NT>Steps</NT> based on their services/tools. This means they have full power to roll out updates to the <NT>Step</NT> while <NT>Bitrise</NT> maintains an overlaying control to ensure service quality and security.

A Verified <NT>Step</NT> means that the owner of a service or tool or an open source team guarantees secure, maintained, consistent, and high-quality performance for any <NT>Bitrise</NT> user. Our official <NT>Bitrise Steps</NT> are maintained by us, whereas our Community <NT>Steps</NT> are maintained by the community. It’s easy to decide which type a <NT>Step</NT> falls into on our GUI.

- <NT>Verified Steps</NT> are labeled with a blue badge in [Bitrise](http://bitrise.io/).
- Official <NT>Bitrise Steps</NT> are labeled with a green badge.
- Community created <NT>Steps</NT> do not have any badge.

We strongly recommend that you consult with our [Step development](/en/bitrise-ci/workflows-and-pipelines/developing-your-own-bitrise-step/developing-a-new-step) guidelines before creating a <NT>Step</NT>.


## Managing contributions

The following guidelines aim to help Verified <NT>Step</NT> authors categorize contributions. The Verified <NT>Step</NT> author is responsible for any contribution made to the Verified <NT>Step</NT>. The Verified <NT>Step</NT> author acknowledges the contribution by adding a label an estimated time to perform the fix, and merge the PR. There are four labels the author can use to categorize the type of contribution:

- `critical-bug` label means that the current feature set has abnormal behavior, which blocks users from using the <NT>Step</NT> and there is no workaround to fix the issue. This critical bug must be fixed by the author.
- `bug` label means that the current feature set has abnormal behavior, which does not block users from using the <NT>Step</NT> and there is a workaround for the issue. This bug must be fixed by the author.
- `feature-request` label means that a new feature or <NT>Step</NT> is being requested. The Verified <NT>Step</NT> author can decide if the feature is worth implementing.
- `maintenance` label means improving the <NT>Step</NT>’s source code in a way that it does not add new features or potential bugs to the <NT>Step</NT>. The Verified <NT>Step</NT> author can decide whether the feature is worth implementing or not.
- `rejected` label means the contribution which gets rejected by the Verified <NT>Step</NT> author must be closed within the first response time, that is 5 business days. When rejecting a contribution, the Verified <NT>Step</NT> author has to provide an explanation to the contributor within the first response time.
- `accepted` contribution means that the given: critical-bug, bug, feature, maintenance will be fixed/merged within the given resolution time.

First response time means that there is a 5 day window during which the Verified <NT>Step</NT> author should respond to the contribution with the accepted or rejected labels.

Resolution time means a certain amount of business days during which the contribution (issue or PR) should be completed by the Verified <NT>Step</NT> author.

| Type | First response time | Resolution time |
| --- | --- | --- |
| critical-bug | 5 business days | 10 business days |
| bug | 5 business days | 15 business days |
| feature-request | 5 business days | 20 business days |
| maintenance | 5 business days | 20 business days |


## How do we go about <NT>Step</NT> duplications?

In general we try to keep our StepLib streamlined and avoid <NT>Step</NT> duplications for the same build task. Here you can find some questions and answers when it comes to any potential <NT>Step</NT> duplications.

- *I was going to submit a <NT>Step</NT> and apply for the Verified badge, but found out there was an official <NT>Bitrise Step</NT> for the same build task in the StepLib. What should I do?*

Submit your <NT>Step</NT> and go through the application process. Once your application is completed, we deprecate the official <NT>Bitrise Step</NT> and our users can use your new Verified <NT>Step</NT>.

- *I was going to submit a <NT>Step</NT> and apply for the Verified badge, but found out there was a Community <NT>Step</NT> for the same build task. What should I do?*

Submit your <NT>Step</NT> and go through the application process. Your new Verified <NT>Step</NT> and the Community <NT>Step</NT> will be both available in our StepLib.

- *I was going to submit a Community <NT>Step</NT> but found out there was a Verified <NT>Step</NT> for the same build task. What should I do?*

If a Verified <NT>Step</NT> is already available in our StepLib, we reject Community <NT>Step</NT> submission for the same build task to avoid <NT>Step</NT> duplication. We offer to the Community <NT>Step</NT> developer to work on future updates of the already existing Verified <NT>Step</NT>.


## Our requests to verified <NT>Step</NT> maintainers

We ask verified <NT>Step</NT> maintainers to follow our best practices:

- Monitor issues opened by users in your <NT>Step</NT> repository and respond in a timely manner.
- Be familiar with our [Stack update policy](/en/bitrise-build-hub/infrastructure/build-stacks/macos-stack-update-policy), especially the cadence of breaking changes. Your <NT>Step</NT> might (accidentally or on purpose) depend on tools from the stack environment, and we don’t want <NT>Steps</NT> to be broken after stack updates.
- Test your <NT>Steps</NT> regularly on all available stacks and platforms. We recommend setting up CI workflows with scheduled builds and some form of notification if the build fails.

If you need any help with <NT>Step</NT>-related questions, feel free to reach out! For example, we're happy to help with:

- Best practices for performing common tasks in <NT>Steps</NT>.
- Best practices for <NT>Step inputs</NT> and outputs, chaining <NT>Steps</NT>.
- Troubleshooting <NT>Step</NT> errors.
