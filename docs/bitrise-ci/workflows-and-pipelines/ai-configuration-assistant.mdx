<NT>Bitrise</NT> offers hundreds of integrations for all CI/CD purposes, allowing complex workflows. It takes time to become familiar with our <NT><GlossTerm baseform="step">Step</GlossTerm></NT>s, our <NT><GlossTerm baseform="stack">stack</GlossTerm></NT>s, or the intricacies of <NT><GlossTerm baseform="configuration yaml">configuration YAML</GlossTerm></NT> - time that you could spend developing your app. To speed up the process, we introduced the AI configuration assistant.

The AI config assistant is a chat-based assistant built into the <NT>Bitrise</NT> <NT><GlossTerm baseform="workflow editor">Workflow Editor</GlossTerm></NT> that turns natural-language descriptions into validated YAML. You describe what you want in plain language: for example, "build an iOS app with Fastlane and send artifacts to <NT>Slack</NT>". The assistant produces a working <NT><GlossTerm baseform="workflow">Workflow</GlossTerm></NT> or <NT><GlossTerm baseform="pipeline">Pipeline</GlossTerm></NT> that you can refine and iterate on either with the help of the assistant or manually.

The assistant can also explain, in plain language, a given <NT>Workflow</NT> or <NT>Pipeline</NT>, including what each <NT>Step</NT> does. It can also suggest improvements. You can request modifications and improvements for an already existing <NT>Workflow</NT> or <NT>Pipeline</NT> even if it was created manually.


## Enabling the AI configuration assistant

The AI configuration assistant requires two separate toggles to be enabled: one at the <NT>workspace</NT> level and one at the project level.

1. <Partial_OpeningTheWorkspaceSettingsPage />
1. Select **<NT>AI settings</NT>**.
1. <NT>Enable the</NT> **<NT>Enable AI features</NT>** toggle. This turns on <NT>Bitrise AI</NT> for the <NT>workspace</NT>. Individual AI features still need to be enabled separately in each project's settings.

   ![Workspace settings — AI settings page with the Enable AI features toggle](/img/workflows-and-pipelines/2026-07-03-workspace-settings-ai-settings.png)

1. Open your project on the <NT>Bitrise CI</NT> page and click **<NT>Project settings</NT>**.
1. Select **<NT>Bitrise AI</NT>** in the left navigation.
1. Under **<NT>Workflows and pipelines</NT>**, <NT>enable the</NT> **<NT>Configuration generator</NT>** toggle.

   ![Project settings — Bitrise AI page with the Configuration generator toggle enabled](/img/workflows-and-pipelines/2026-07-03-project-settings-bitrise-ai-configuration-generator.png)

:::note[AI credits]

AI features consume monthly AI credits. The number of credits available depends on your plan. You can check your remaining credits on the **<NT>Bitrise AI</NT>** page in <NT>Project settings</NT>.

:::

Once both toggles are enabled, the AI configuration assistant is available in the <NT>Workflow Editor</NT>.


## Explaining a <NT>Workflow</NT> or a <NT>Pipeline</NT>

You can get the AI assistant to explain how any part of your <NT>Bitrise</NT> configuration works and it can suggest improvements to existing <NT>Workflows</NT> and <NT>Pipelines</NT>.

To get an explanation, open a <NT>Workflow Editor</NT> and select a <NT>Workflow</NT> or <NT>Pipeline</NT>. Next to its name, click **<NT>Explain</NT>**. The AI assistant will open with a prompt already submitted. For example, if you click the button for a <NT>Workflow</NT> called **test**, the prompt will be **<NT>Explain</NT> 'test' workflow**.

The explanation aims to cover all parts of the configuration: the <NT><GlossTerm baseform="trigger">trigger</GlossTerm></NT>s and the <NT>Environment Variables</NT> belonging to the <NT>Workflow</NT>, the infrastructure, including the stack and the machine type, and the <NT>Steps</NT>, each with its own explanation. The assistant also provides information about the usage of the <NT>Workflow</NT> in other contests: for example, whether it's part of a <NT>Pipeline</NT> and what function it serves there.


## Creating a <NT>Workflow</NT> or <NT>Pipeline</NT> with the AI assistant

Use the <NT>Bitrise AI</NT> configuration assistant to create a fully validated, working <NT>Workflow</NT> or <NT>Pipeline</NT>. Use plain language to list your requirements and the AI will create a configuration that you can modify at any point. It will be available in the <NT>Workflow Editor</NT> and in your configuration YAML file just as any other <NT>Workflow</NT> or <NT>Pipeline</NT>.

1. Open the <NT>Workflow Editor</NT>.
1. On the top bar, click **<NT>Ask AI</NT>**.
1. In the **<NT>Assistant</NT>** window, select **<NT>Create a new Workflow</NT>**.

   :::note

   You can also open the <NT>Workflow</NT> selector dropdown and click **<NT>Create Workflow with AI</NT>** at the bottom of the list.

   :::

1. Add your prompt in the input field.

   Use plain language to explain what you need the <NT>Workflow</NT> or <NT>Pipeline</NT> to do. You don't have to use <NT>Step</NT> names or <NT>Bitrise</NT>-specific terms. The more detailed your prompt is, the better the AI output.

   :::note

   While the AI assistant is working, you can modify your configuration manually. However, your changes will be overwritten when the assistant is ready and you apply its changes.

   :::
1. Ask for changes if you need them. You can keep iterating.
1. When finished, click **<NT>Apply changes</NT>**. This overwrites your configuration YAML file.
