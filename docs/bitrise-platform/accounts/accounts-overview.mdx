When you sign up for <NT>Bitrise</NT>, you create your own individual user account. By itself, having a user account isn’t enough to add projects and run builds. For that, you must be part of a <NT>workspace</NT> or invited to a project as an outside contributor.

You can connect your personal account to:

- Your Git provider accounts via OAuth, allowing you to log in to <NT>Bitrise</NT> through your Git provider and to configure repository access for <NT>Bitrise CI</NT>: [Repository access with OAuth](/en/bitrise-platform/repository-access/repository-access-with-oauth).
- The <NT>Bitrise</NT> <NT>GitHub</NT> App, allowing authentication and repository access without the need for SSH keys: [GitHub app integration](/en/bitrise-platform/repository-access/github-app-integration).
- <NT>Your Apple ID</NT>, allowing you to connect <NT>Bitrise</NT> <NT>project</NT>s to an <NT>Apple</NT> service: [Connecting to an Apple service with Apple ID](/en/bitrise-platform/integrations/apple-services-connection/connecting-to-an-apple-service-with-apple-id).

## <NT>Account settings</NT> page

Your <NT>Bitrise</NT> account is managed from your [Account settings page](http://app.bitrise.io/me/account). From this page, you can:

- [Edit your profile](/en/bitrise-platform/accounts/editing-your-profile-settings), including username, email address, password, and avatar.
- <NT>Set up</NT> [Apple ID connection](/en/bitrise-platform/integrations/apple-services-connection/connecting-to-an-apple-service-with-apple-id#adding-apple-id-authentication-data-on-bitrise).
- [Enable two-factor authentication](/en/bitrise-platform/accounts/two-factor-authentication#enabling-two-factor-authentication).
- Connect [LaunchDarkly feature flags](/en/release-management/configuring-connected-apps/integrating-launchdarkly-feature-flags) for <NT>Release Management</NT>.
- Create and manage personal access tokens used to access the [Bitrise API](/en/bitrise-ci/api/api-overview).
- [Register test devices](/en/bitrise-ci/testing/testing-ios-apps/registering-a-test-device).

## SAML SSO

You can log in to <NT>Bitrise</NT> via SAML SSO. <NT>Bitrise</NT> supports multiple SAML identity providers. To log in via SAML SSO:

- Your <NT>workspace</NT> must have SAML SSO enabled.
- Make sure the email address belonging to your personal <NT>Bitrise</NT> account is also registered to your SAML identity provider.

For detailed information on how to set it up, see: [Configuring SAML SSO on Bitrise](/en/bitrise-platform/accounts/saml-sso-in-bitrise/configuring-saml-sso-on-bitrise).
