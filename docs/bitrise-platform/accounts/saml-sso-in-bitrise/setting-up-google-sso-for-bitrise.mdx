This guide provides step-by-step instructions on setting up <NT>Bitrise</NT> as a SAML application on Google <NT><GlossTerm baseform="workspace">Workspace</GlossTerm></NT>.

<Partial_CAUTIONSAMLSSORestrictions />

Before connecting SAML SSO to your <NT>Workspace</NT>:

- Make sure you have a Google administrator account where you can add <NT>Bitrise</NT> as a SAML app. The Google <NT>Workspace</NT> administrator can help setting up SAML SSO on Google <NT>Workspace</NT>.
- <Partial_SAMLSSOBeforeYouStart />

You'll need to:

1. [Get configuration information from Google Workspace and Bitrise.](#getting-configuration-information-from-google-workspace-and-bitrise)
1. [Enable the Bitrise app for a group or an organizational unit.](#enabling-bitrise-app-for-a-group-or-an-organizational-unit)


## Getting configuration information from Google <NT>Workspace</NT> and <NT>Bitrise</NT>

1. Sign into your Google Admin Console.
1. Select **<NT>Apps</NT>** on the **Admin Console** page.

   ![googleadmin-apps.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-d00d79a9-94d7-7bf0-fa17-b6e3adab0619.jpg)
1. On the <NT>Apps</NT> page, click **SAML apps**.

   ![appsamlapp.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-6cf46631-6ee5-1108-8957-9da55ebd564b.jpg)
1. On the **Web and mobile apps** page, click the **<NT>Add</NT> apps** button, and select the **<NT>Add</NT> custom SAML app** option from the dropdown.

   ![addappsaml.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-bcfdf2cb-0157-100c-44ed-25e040d82e6b.jpg)
1. On the App details page add `Bitrise` as your app name. Please note it must be `Bitrise` as no other format is accepted. Click **<NT>Continue</NT>**.

   ![addappname.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-1a730b40-ecbe-d3dd-58a4-c46a0854f63a.jpg)
1. On the **Getting Identity Provider details** page:

   - Copy the **<NT>SSO URL</NT>** and paste it on the **<NT>SAML SSO</NT> provider Single Sing-On URL (SSO URL)** on <NT>Bitrise</NT>.
   - Copy the whole content of the **Certificate** field and paste it in the **<NT>SAML SSO provider certificate</NT>** field of <NT>Bitrise</NT>. You can upload the **Certificate** from your local computer too.

     ![identityproviderdetails.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-21e11866-5b32-ba61-b232-0336d27e75e9.jpg)
1. While you are in <NT>Bitrise</NT>, click the **<NT>Copy</NT> Link** button to copy the **Assertion Consumer Service URL (ACS URL)**. We will need it on Google <NT>Workspace</NT> in a second. Let’s NOT click the **<NT>Configure SSO</NT>** button just now!
1. Let’s head back to the **Getting Identity Provider details** page of Google <NT>Workspace</NT>. Click **<NT>Continue</NT>**.
1. On the **<NT>Service</NT> provider details** page:

   - Paste the **Assertion Consumer Service URL (ACS URL)** of <NT>Bitrise</NT> in the **ACS URL** field on Google <NT>Workspace</NT>. (Remember, we got the link at <NT><GlossTerm baseform="step">Step</GlossTerm></NT> 7.)
   - Type `Bitrise` in the **Entity ID** field. Please note it must be `Bitrise` as no other format is accepted.
   - Tick the **Signed response** checkbox under **<NT>START</NT> URL (optional)**.
   - Click **<NT>CONTINUE</NT>**.

     ![serviceproviderdetails.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-dca4e3b0-8c11-d444-23c9-b5ae0f21990d.jpg)
1. Click the **<NT>Configure SSO</NT>** button on <NT>Bitrise</NT>.
1. On Google <NT>Workspace</NT>’s **Attribute mapping** page, click **<NT>Finish</NT>** - you do not have to configure anything here.


## Enabling <NT>Bitrise</NT> app for a group or an organizational unit

All there is left to do on G Suite is to enable the newly created <NT>Bitrise</NT> app for a group or organization of your choice.

1. Go to the **Web and mobile apps** page on G <NT>Suite</NT> and select **<NT>Bitrise</NT>** from the **<NT>Apps</NT>** list.
1. Click **User access** to get to the **<NT>Service</NT> status** page.
1. Select **ON for everyone** and hit **<NT>Save</NT>**.

   ![useraccess.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-ed1aa9a4-7926-3cfe-bf98-ac0f98a44237.jpg)
