This guide provides step-by-step instructions on setting up SAML SSO using [Microsoft Active Directory Federation Services](https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-server/identity/active-directory-federation-services) (<NT>AD FS</NT>).

<Partial_CAUTIONSAMLSSORestrictions />

Before connecting SAML SSO to your <NT><GlossTerm baseform="workspace">Workspace</GlossTerm></NT>:

- Make sure the <NT>AD FS</NT> administrator is at hand during the SAML SSO configuration process.
- <Partial_SAMLSSOBeforeYouStart />

In this tutorial we will be jumping back and forth between <NT>Bitrise</NT> and <NT>AD FS</NT> so it is recommended that both tools are available during this process.

To configure SAML SSO with <NT>AD FS</NT>, you'll need to:

1. [Add the **Identity provider sign-on URL** from AD FS on Bitrise](#adding-the-identity-provider-sign-on-url).
1. [Export a certificate generated by AD FS and add it on Bitrise](#exporting-an-ad-fs-certificate).
1. [Add Bitrise as a relying party trust to AD FS](#adding-bitrise-as-a-relying-party-trust-to-ad-fs).
1. [Configure claim rules](#configuring-claim-rules).


## Adding the identity provider sign-on URL

1. <Partial_OpeningTheWorkspaceSettingsPage />
1. Go to the **<NT>Single Sign-on</NT>** tab.
1. Add the **Identity provider sign-on URL** from <NT>AD FS</NT> in the **<NT>SAML SSO</NT> provider <NT>Single Sign-On</NT> URL (SSO URL)** field.

   For example, a valid value is `https://<AD FS URL>.com/adfs/ls`.


## Exporting an <NT>AD FS</NT> certificate

1. <NT>You have</NT> to add a certificate generated by <NT>AD FS</NT> to the **<NT>SAML SSO provider certificate</NT>** field of the **<NT>Single Sign-On</NT>** page on <NT>Bitrise</NT>. If you’ve already created a certificate on <NT>AD FS</NT>, you can export it in PEM format from the <NT>AD FS</NT> server. If you haven’t created one yet, follow the instructions: [Obtain and Configure TS and TD Certificates for AD FS](https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-server/identity/ad-fs/operations/configure-ts-td-certs-ad-fs#:\~:text=Open%20the%20AD%20FS%20Management,certificates%2C%20and%20then%20click%20OK.).
1. In **Server <NT>Manager</NT>**, click **<NT>Tool</NT>s**, and select **<NT>AD FS</NT> Management**.
1. Select the **<NT>Certificates</NT>** folder on the left menu pane.
1. Click a certificate under **<NT>Token</NT>-signing**. This brings up the **<NT>Certificates</NT>** window.

   ![certtoken-1.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-cfe05e2a-2027-50fb-e4a8-56ef4164907f.jpg)
1. Click **<NT>Details</NT>** tab on the **Certificate** page.

   ![certificate-1.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-e960a587-dfa0-3b5d-aad8-6d03208a3ad6.jpg)
1. Hit **<NT>Next</NT>** on the **Certificate Export Wizard** window.

   ![certwizard.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-0ba8034b-4784-e042-02b9-32087bcde8d4.jpg)
1. Select the **Base-64 encoded X.509 (.CER)** the export file format. Click **<NT>Next</NT>**.

   ![baseencoded.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-9a300011-7818-826c-5874-c7ba6ade8176.jpg)
1. Give it a name in the **File name** field and hit **<NT>Save</NT>**.

   ![filenamesave.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-4510e204-8973-1a9a-0c2e-7ffc0c073aad.jpg)
1. Have a final look at your certificate settings. If you need to modify any of those, click the backward arrow next to **Certificate Export Wizard**. Otherwise, click **<NT>Finish</NT>**. Make sure you leave the <NT>AD FS</NT> window open as you will need it in a minute.

   ![completewizard.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-eaa066c4-99be-c8a5-9990-fe4a08a4154c.jpg)
1. Open the exported certificate by a text editor and copy/paste its content to the **<NT>SAML SSO provider certificate</NT>** field or upload the file itself from your local computer.
1. Save the settings by clicking **<NT>Configure SSO</NT>** on <NT>Bitrise</NT>.

   ![saml_sso_setup.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-26ad84be-65c7-1916-f079-6b553b4c436d.png)

   Let’s continue the SAML SSO configuration on <NT>AD FS</NT> by adding <NT>Bitrise</NT>.


## Adding <NT>Bitrise</NT> as a <NT>relying party trust</NT> to <NT>AD FS</NT>

Once you are finished with exporting the certificate, you can continue with adding <NT>Bitrise</NT> as a [relying party trust to AD FS](https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-server/identity/ad-fs/operations/create-a-relying-party-trust). The Add <NT>Relying Party Trust</NT> Wizard guides you through the steps.

1. On <NT>AD FS</NT>, click **<NT>Relying Party Trust</NT>** on the left menu bar, then click **<NT>Relying Party Trust</NT>**.
1. Select **<NT>Add</NT> <NT>Relying Party Trust</NT>** under **<NT>Actions</NT>**.

   ![addreplyingpartytrust.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-2e8a2cfc-6fe9-cf2b-9b4a-b4ec2f051568.jpg)
1. On the **Welcome** page, select the **Claims aware** option and hit **<NT>Start</NT>**.

   ![claimsaware.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-d5eac9a8-1c74-efd6-459a-aaa24af4877a.jpg)
1. On the **<NT>Select</NT> Data <NT>Source</NT>** page, click the **Enter data about the relying party manually** option on the bottom of the page. Click **<NT>Next</NT>**.

   ![selectdatasource.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-7e451b81-22e3-0f16-af91-380ad8026de1.jpg)
1. On the **Specify <NT>Display Name</NT>** page, add a **<NT>Display name</NT>,** for example `MyCorp`. Click **<NT>Next</NT>**.

   ![specifydisplayname.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-a8509ed5-9cc9-8851-c8df-9d298c5ae3bc.jpg)
1. Specify a token encryption certificate on the **<NT>Configure</NT> Certificate** page is optional. Click **<NT>Next</NT>**.

   ![optionalconfigure.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-1042aa27-f21e-16e5-4065-4088112ae116.jpg)
1. On the **<NT>Configure</NT> UR**L page, select **<NT>Enable</NT> support for the SAML 2.0 WebSSO protocol** and copy paste the **Assertion Consumer Service URL (ACS URL)** from <NT>Bitrise</NT> to the **Relying party SAML 2. 0 SSO service URL** field on <NT>AD FS</NT>. Click **<NT>Next</NT>**.

   ![configureurl-1.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-a40d7228-e976-458e-0508-99ded152069c.jpg)
1. On the **<NT>Configure</NT> Identifiers** page, add `Bitrise` in the **<NT>Relying party trust</NT> identifier** field. Click **<NT>Add</NT>**, then hit **<NT>Next</NT>**.

   ![replyingidentifiers2.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-c920feb2-3a96-88d7-710a-27d3d4fcba1c.jpg)
1. Do not modify the default access control policy on the **Choose Access Control Policy** page so that everyone can access this SAML SSO connection. Click **<NT>Next</NT>**.

   ![permiteveryone.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-d6600b5b-3ad8-aee2-a28b-62f4ca2b1394.jpg)
1. On the **Ready to <NT>Add</NT> Trust** page, review the settings and click **<NT>Next</NT>**.

   ![readytoaddtrust.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-4033d0af-df38-8e77-9963-4812d259f9ad.jpg)
1. On the **<NT>Finish</NT>** page, tick the checkbox to edit claims issuance policy for <NT>Bitrise</NT>. Click **<NT>Close</NT>**.

   ![finish.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-974f5427-db6a-201d-a112-9bb8fee4ae7f.jpg)

1. On <NT>AD FS</NT>, click **<NT>Relying Party Trust</NT>** on the left menu bar, then click **<NT>Relying Party Trust</NT>**.
1. Select **<NT>Add</NT> <NT>Relying Party Trust</NT>** under **<NT>Actions</NT>**.

## Configuring claim rules

1. On the **<NT>Edit</NT> Claim** **Issuance Policy** page, click the **<NT>Add</NT> Rule** button and hit **OK**.

   ![editclaims.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-0a57f576-6cbd-5055-e09e-078920e410ce.jpg)
1. Create a **<NT>Send</NT> LDAP Attributes as Claims** claim rule and click **<NT>Next</NT>**.
1. On the **<NT>Configure</NT> Claim Rule** page:

   - Add a rule name, for example Send E-mail, in the **Claim rule name** field.
   - Select an **Attribute <NT>Store</NT>** which is most likely the Active Directory.
   - In the **Mapping of LDAP attributes to outgoing claim types** field select E-mail Addresses.
1. Click **<NT>Finish</NT>**.

   ![configureclaimrule.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-73afe848-4767-6771-43fb-4675ecc1a189.jpg)
1. Add another new rule that turns an E-mail to a formatter NameID. To do so, click **<NT>Add</NT> rule** in the **<NT>Edit</NT> Claim** **Issuance Policy** page again.
1. On the **<NT>Select</NT> Rule Template**, select **Transform an <NT>Incoming</NT> Claim** option in the **Claim rule template** dropdown. Click **<NT>Next</NT>**.

   ![chooseruletype.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-0ef22e95-4303-25ce-b8e9-69884bbe7cf7.jpg)
1. Give a name to the new rule, for example, `Transform E-mail`.
1. Select **<NT>E-Mail Address</NT>** as the **<NT>Incoming</NT> Claim Type**.
1. Select **NameId** as the **Outgoing claim type.**
1. Choose **<NT>Email</NT>** as the **Outgoing name ID format**.
1. Hit **OK** to finish the process.

   ![newrule.jpg](/img/_paligo/uuid-ce7f8183-941b-0b73-92c2-185e235442ee.jpg)
