<NT><GlossTerm baseform="Workspace">Workspace</GlossTerm></NT> members can log in to a <NT>Bitrise</NT> <NT>workspace</NT> using their own SAML SSO provider’s system. With SAML SSO, <NT>workspaces</NT> will be able to apply the security guidelines of their SAML SSO provider when accessing their <NT>Bitrise</NT> <NT>workspace</NT>.

SAML SSO can also be enforced on a <NT>workspace</NT>: enforcing makes SAML SSO the only way for logging in to the <NT>workspace</NT>.


## Verifying your domain

You can add and verify your corporate domains from where you will manage <NT>Bitrise</NT> users. You can have multiple verified domains on <NT>Bitrise</NT> but you can only add one domain at a time. Multiple subdomains of the same domain count as different domains as <NT>Bitrise</NT> expects an exact match.

Verifying your domain is a requirement for configuring SCIM and it makes it much more convenient for users to sign up and log in to <NT>Bitrise</NT> via SAML SSO. We recommend starting your SAML SSO setup with verifying your domain as the process can take some time.

1. <Partial_OpeningTheWorkspaceSettingsPage />
1. On the left, select **<NT>Single sign-on</NT>**.
1. Select the **<NT>Domains</NT>** tab.
1. In the **<NT>Domain control</NT>** section, click **<NT>Add domain</NT>**.
1. Enter <NT>your domain name</NT> in the dialog box, and click **<NT>Next</NT>**.

   :::important[Handling subdomains]

   <NT>Bitrise</NT> expects an exact match for the domain names. If you use subdomains, you need to add the full name of the subdomain. For example, if you own mydomain.com and you want to use its subdomain external.mydomain.com, you should write it out the subdomain's name when setting up domain control on <NT>Bitrise</NT>.

   :::
1. You will see a domain verification code. You need to add this code to as a DNS TXT record at your domain provider.
1. Click **<NT>Copy and close</NT>** to copy the verification code and close the dialog box.

After you've added the DNS TXT record at your domain provider, we'll commence domain verification. This can take up to 72 hours. You will receive an email once it is completed.


## Setting up SAML SSO for a <NT>Bitrise</NT> <NT>workspace</NT>

In this tutorial, we describe how <NT>workspace</NT> owners can set up their SAML SSO and invite <NT>workspace</NT> members to set up their own connections.

Before connecting SAML SSO to your <NT>workspace</NT>, you need:

- A SAML SSO provider (Identity Provider) that you can connect <NT>Bitrise</NT> to and the administrator to the SAML SSO provider is at hand.
- An [owner or manager of the workspace](/en/bitrise-platform/workspaces/collaboration-and-permissions-in-workspaces/workspace-collaboration). <NT>Workspace</NT> members with the role of contributor or viewer can't set up SAML SSO.

To start configuring SAML SSO:

1. <Partial_OpeningTheWorkspaceSettingsPage />
1. Select the **<NT>Single Sign-On</NT>** tab on the left.
1. Click **<NT>Configure</NT> <NT>SAML SSO</NT>**. It opens the **<NT>Setup connection</NT>** screen.
1. <NT>Log in</NT> to your SAML SSO provider and add <NT>Bitrise</NT> as a SAML SSO application, using the values from the **<NT>Setup connection</NT>** screen in your <NT>Bitrise</NT> <NT>workspace settings</NT>:

   ![sso-setup.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-baadaff0-fbae-febb-cca3-077a707b1676.png)

   - Copy the **Assertion Consumer Service URL (ACS URL)** by clicking **<NT>Copy</NT>**. The provider sends the SAML response to the ACS URL. Some providers call it Reply URL, Callback URL, or <NT>Single Sign-On</NT> URL.
   - Copy the **<NT>Single Logout URL</NT>** by clicking **<NT>Copy</NT>**. The provider sends the SAML logout response to the <NT>Single Logout URL</NT>.

   You will receive configuration values from your SAML SSO provider.
1. Add your SAML SSO provider configuration values:

   - **<NT>SSO URL</NT>**: <NT>Bitrise</NT> sends the SAML request to the SSO URL. Some providers call it Login URL.
   - **<NT>SSO Logout URL</NT> (optional)**: <NT>Bitrise</NT> sends the SAML logout request to the <NT>SSO Logout URL</NT>.
   - **<NT>Application Identifier</NT> (optional)**: This is necessary if multiple <NT>workspaces</NT> use the same identity provider. This may be called Entity ID, Audience URI, or something similar.
1. Upload the <NT>SAML SSO provider certificate</NT>.

   You can either upload the file or paste the certificate manually.

   ![sso-certificate.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-e1e79927-bba9-65ca-01a5-24ef83efe442.png)
1. Click **<NT>Save changes</NT>**.

If you’ve completed the steps, you and <NT>workspace</NT> members should get a verification email about SAML SSO connected to the respective <NT>workspace</NT>.


## Checking SAML SSO statuses on <NT>Bitrise</NT>

Now that the <NT>Workspace</NT> owner has set up SAML SSO for the <NT>Workspace</NT>, all <NT>Workspace</NT> members (including the <NT>Workspace</NT> owner) can check their other <NT>Workspace</NT> member’s SAML SSO statuses on the **<NT>Team</NT>** tab.

:::note[Accessing the <NT>Single Sign-on</NT> tab]

The **<NT>Single Sign-On</NT>** tab is only available for the <NT>Workspace</NT> owner.

:::

There are two kinds of SAML SSO statuses on <NT>Bitrise</NT>.

- **SAML IS ENABLED:** Login via SAML SSO is enabled.
- **SAML IS DISABLED:** The <NT>Workspace</NT> member has not enabled the SAML SSO connection yet. To enable it, the <NT>Workspace</NT> member has to follow the instructions in the verification email from <NT>Bitrise</NT>.

1. Go to your <NT>Workspace</NT>’s profile page.
1. Select **<NT>Team</NT>** from the left menu.
1. Go to the <NT>Members</NT> tab to check the <NT>Workspace</NT> member’s SAML status.

   ![saml_status.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-38cefb5d-2dc6-7a86-98cd-d1dac6484ded.png)


## Enforcing SAML SSO on a <NT>Workspace</NT>

Enforcing SAML SSO on your <NT>Workspace</NT> provides an extra layer of security: you can enforce your own security guidelines to your <NT>Bitrise</NT> <NT>Workspace</NT> (for example, password format requirements, two-factor authentication).

:::warning[Enforced SAML SSO]

Enforcing SAML SSO in your <NT>Workspace</NT> makes SAML SSO the only way for logging in/signing up to the <NT>Workspace</NT>.

:::

:::caution[One <NT>Workspace</NT> only]

You cannot be a member in two <NT>Workspaces</NT> that enforced SAML SSO on <NT>Bitrise</NT>.

:::

1. <Partial_OpeningTheWorkspaceSettingsPage />
1. Go to your <NT>Workspace</NT>’s **Single Sign On** tab.
1. Check the **<NT>Enforce SAML SSO</NT>** checkbox to enforce SAML SSO.

   ![enforce_saml.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-642c1745-3bb7-c296-bd5a-35a2368a3945.png)

   :::note[Unable to enforce SAML SSO]

   A <NT>Workspace</NT> owner cannot enforce SAML SSO on the <NT>Workspace</NT> if <NT>Workspace</NT> members have not enabled their SAML SSO connection yet or they enabled SAML SSO with another <NT>Workspace</NT> that enforces SAML SSO.

   :::
1. Click **<NT>Save changes</NT>**.

Now <NT>Workspace</NT> members can only log in via SAML SSO.


## Adding a new user to a <NT>workspace</NT> with enforced SAML SSO

You can add new users to a <NT>workspace</NT> with enforced SAML SSO. There are three cases:

- The <NT>workspace</NT> has a verified domain: [Verified domain](/en/bitrise-platform/accounts/saml-sso-in-bitrise/configuring-saml-sso-on-bitrise#verifying-your-domain). You can invite users with the same email domain as you would to any other <NT>workspace</NT>: [Workspace collaboration](/en/bitrise-platform/workspaces/collaboration-and-permissions-in-workspaces/workspace-collaboration).
- The <NT>workspace</NT> doesn't have a verified domain and you want to invite a user who hasn't registered a <NT>Bitrise</NT> account yet: invite the user as you would to any other <NT>workspace</NT>.
- The <NT>workspace</NT> doesn't have a verified domain and you want to invite a user with an existing <NT>Bitrise</NT> account: turn off [enforced SAML SSO](/en/bitrise-platform/accounts/saml-sso-in-bitrise/configuring-saml-sso-on-bitrise#enforcing-saml-sso-on-a-workspace), invite the user, and turn enforcement back on.

| Domain verification status | New user | Existing user |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Verified domain | Simple invite | Simple invite |
| Non-verified domain | Simple invite | Turn off SAML SSO enforcement  Invite the user  Turn on SAML SSO enforcement again |


## Disabling a <NT>Workspace</NT>'s SAML SSO

If you disable SAML SSO, <NT>Workspace</NT> members will be able to sign in with the regular sign-in procedure.

1. <Partial_OpeningTheWorkspaceSettingsPage />
1. Go to the **<NT>Single Sign-On</NT>** tab.
1. If SAML SSO has been enforced on the <NT>Workspace</NT> before, toggle **<NT>Enforce</NT>** under **<NT>Enforce SAML SSO</NT>** off.
1. Click **<NT>Disable SSO</NT>**.

   A confirmation pop-up appears where you can confirm/cancel your action. Please note that by clicking the **<NT>Disable SSO</NT>** button, you will disable SAML SSO for all <NT>Workspace</NT> members. Once it’s done, <NT>Workspace</NT> members will be able to log in through their normal <NT>Bitrise</NT> credentials.

   ![disable_sso.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-ea11f723-edcb-2e88-0d83-c4c60f1cb794.png)

You will receive an **SSO has been disabled** email from <NT>Bitrise</NT> (letsconnect@bitrise.io) which confirms the disabled SAML SSO for the <NT>Workspace</NT>.


## Disabling a <NT>Workspace</NT> member's SAML SSO

If you are a <NT>Workspace</NT> owner, you can disable a <NT>Workspace</NT> member’s SAML SSO connection to the <NT>Workspace</NT> on <NT>Bitrise</NT>. There are two ways to do so:

- Remove the user from the <NT>Workspace</NT>.
- Remove the user from the SAML SSO provider which means the user would not be able to log in with SAML SSO any more.


## Updating SAML SSO configuration

You can update a <NT>Workspace</NT>’s configured SAML SSO using the **<NT>Configure SAML SSO provider</NT>** button on the **<NT>Single Sign-On</NT>** page.

This comes in handy if your SAML SSO provider’s certificate has expired and you wish to insert the new certificate on <NT>Bitrise</NT>. Another use case is, for example, if SAML SSO has been configured a while ago and now you wish to check the current configuration details.

:::important[Accessing the Update SSO button]

As with other SAML SSO configurations, only the <NT>Workspace</NT> owner can access and use the Update SSO button.

:::

1. As the owner of the <NT>Workspace</NT>, click your <NT>Workspace</NT>’s **<NT>Single Sign-On</NT>** tab.
1. Click the **<NT>Configure SAML SSO provider</NT>** button. Now you can access the configuration details of <NT>Workspace</NT>’s SAML SSO.

   ![configure_update_sso.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-2b97d7ef-4512-cf79-868f-bc66e728b509.png)
1. Make the changes and click **<NT>Save changes</NT>**.

   From now on any SAML SSO request will use the new configuration automatically.
