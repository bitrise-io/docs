In this article we describe how you can enable the **<NT>Bitrise Support</NT> Access** so that our Support team can have access to your <NT><GlossTerm baseform="Project">project</GlossTerm></NT>, specifically your <NT><GlossTerm baseform="Workflow">Workflow</GlossTerm></NT>, build log, <NT>project settings</NT> or your `<GlossTerm baseform="bitrise.yml">bitrise.yml</GlossTerm>` file. With the toggle function, you can easily turn the **<NT>Bitrise Support</NT> Access** on and off. No need to add us as a user to your project's Team.

The <NT>Bitrise</NT> Support user, when enabled, has Admin access to your project. That means it can do anything that a regular user with Admin access rights on a project can do: it has access to your builds and <NT>Workflows</NT>, to all the settings in the <NT><GlossTerm baseform="workflow editor">Workflow Editor</GlossTerm></NT> and to the **<NT>Project settings</NT>** page.

:::important[No access to billing information]

The <NT>Bitrise</NT> Support user can’t see your **<NT>Account</NT> information** or any **<NT>Billing</NT>** information. Only the owner of the account has access to this information and has the right to modify any account-related records.

The Support user can’t see your other projects where the Support user is not enabled. For details, see [What the Bitrise Support user can/can't do?](https://support.bitrise.io/hc/en-us/articles/4405741488017)

:::

:::note[How long does the <NT>Bitrise</NT> Support Access remain active?]

Due to security reasons once you toggle the <NT>Bitrise</NT> Support Access on, it will remain active for two weeks after which it automatically gets revoked.

:::

Let’s see how to set it up!

1. <Partial_GettingToTheProjectSettingsPage />
1. On the left, select **<NT>Basic settings</NT>** from the menu options.
1. Scroll down to the **<NT>Support Access</NT>** and toggle **<NT>Grant temporary access</NT>**. It might take a couple of seconds to work and you might need to refresh your page to see the enabled status.

In case of a failing <NT>Workflow</NT>, our best practice is to create a new and correct version of the failing <NT>Workflow</NT> called `support-testing`. You can compare our `support-testing` with your own and update yours or keep the `support-testing` <NT>Workflow</NT>, rename it as you wish, and develop it further.
