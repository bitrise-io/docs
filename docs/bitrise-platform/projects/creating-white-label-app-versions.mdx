This guide explains how to create different versions of your white label project and build all or just one version by chaining <NT><GlossTerm baseform="Workflow">Workflow</GlossTerm></NT>s and setting differences in [Environment Variables](/en/bitrise-ci/configure-builds/environment-variables).

In this article, we’re building three individual versions (red, green, white) of our white label project.

What you’ll need for this setup:

- A main <NT>Workflow</NT> that launches each version-specific <NT>Workflow</NT>.
- One or more version-specific <NT>Workflow</NT>(s) where you can set all the parameters that distinguish your versions from each other.
- A utility <NT>Workflow</NT> which describes your build logic, and refers to the version-specific options as parameters. [Utility Workflows are Workflows that have an underscore before their Workflow ID](/en/bitrise-ci/workflows-and-pipelines/workflows/managing-workflows#utility-workflows). Utility <NT>Workflows</NT> cannot be executed with the `bitrise_run` command: you need to reference them with the `before_run` or `after_run` properties. You can [chain utility Workflows with your regular Workflows](/en/bitrise-ci/workflows-and-pipelines/workflows/managing-workflows#chaining-workflows-together) in the <NT><GlossTerm baseform="Workflow Editor">Workflow Editor</GlossTerm></NT>.


## Prepping <NT>Workflows</NT> of a white label app

1. [Add your project](/en/bitrise-ci/getting-started/adding-a-new-project) to <NT>Bitrise</NT> in the usual way.
1. Select your project on the [Bitrise CI](https://app.bitrise.io/dashboard/) page and click **<NT>Workflows</NT>**.

   ![getting-to-workflows.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-32b5ff3d-8431-8fe3-9971-89d8aca076f4.png)
1. Open the dropdown menu of available <NT>Workflows</NT> and click **<NT>Create</NT> <NT>Workflow</NT>** to create your main <NT>Workflow</NT>.

   ![create-workflow.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-426d42b4-ca6f-d5de-d91a-b9e8a4fd8ba1.png)
1. Add a new <NT>Workflow</NT> based on an **Empty <NT>Workflow</NT>**.

   In this example, our main <NT>Workflow</NT> is called **allcolor**. This <NT>Workflow</NT> will start running your version-specific <NT>Workflows</NT> in the chain.
1. Click **+** again to create your version-specific <NT>Workflow</NT>.

   Keep adding as many <NT>Workflows</NT> as many different versions you wish to create.
1. Select **<NT>Env Vars</NT>** on the left navigation menu and add your version-specific parameters to each <NT>Workflow</NT> you’ve just created.

   In this example we’re adding <NT>Workflow</NT> <NT>Environment Variables</NT> to our **green**, **red** and **white** <NT>Workflows</NT> but leaving **allcolor** intact.

   ![env-ars-whitelabel.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-68837c3f-707c-19aa-86e2-bbac732be5a5.png)
1. <NT>Go back</NT> to **<NT>Workflows</NT>** and create a [utility Workflow](/en/bitrise-ci/workflows-and-pipelines/workflows/managing-workflows#utility-workflows).

   Make sure you give a name that starts with an underscore, for example, **_runner**, otherwise <NT>Bitrise CLI</NT> will not treat it as a utility <NT>Workflow</NT>.
1. Add <NT><GlossTerm baseform="step">Step</GlossTerm></NT>s to your utility <NT>Workflow</NT>.

   In this example, we’re adding a **Script** <NT>Step</NT> which will inherit the Environment Variable from the <NT>Workflows</NT> and print out the value in the build log.

   ![script-param.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-52def735-231b-d0ea-e01e-b8b12cfbe86a.png)


## Chaining <NT>Workflows</NT> for a white label app

Now that we have a bunch of <NT>Workflows</NT> ready, it’s time to chain them together in the right order.

1. Select your main <NT>Workflow</NT> (**allcolor** in this example). This is the <NT>Workflow</NT> that should be triggered by your [build triggers](/en/bitrise-ci/run-and-analyze-builds/build-triggers/configuring-build-triggers).
1. Click the options menu (⋮) next to the <NT>Workflow</NT>'s name and select **<NT>Chain Workflows</NT>**. <NT>Select</NT> a version <NT>Workflow</NT> (for example, **white**) and click **<NT>Add after</NT>**.
1. Add each version-specific <NT>Workflow</NT> the same way (such as white, green, and red in our example) and add your utility <NT>Workflow</NT> after each of them, as seen in this image:

   ![chained-workflows.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-dada2b23-2bc7-5796-3d98-9131dbaa2a87.png)
1. <NT>Go back</NT> to your <NT>Build</NT>’s page and click **<NT>Start build</NT>**.
1. In the **<NT>Build</NT> configuration** dialog, select your main <NT>Workflow</NT> under **<NT>Workflow</NT>**. This will kickstart your chained <NT>Workflows</NT> and build the app versions of your white label app.
