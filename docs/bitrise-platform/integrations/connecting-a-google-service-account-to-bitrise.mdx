You can connect a Google Console Service account to a <NT><GlossTerm baseform="workspace">Workspace</GlossTerm></NT>. The service account will be available to use for all the projects owned by that <NT>Workspace</NT>. It allows you to seamlessly deploy your <NT>Android</NT> apps to <NT>Google Play</NT> with [Release Management](/en/release-management).

You can add multiple service accounts to a <NT>Workspace</NT> but a <NT>Bitrise</NT> <NT><GlossTerm baseform="project">project</GlossTerm></NT> can only have a single service account connected to it at any given time. However, you can change the <NT>connected account</NT> at any time.

## Connecting a Google service account to a <NT>Workspace</NT>

To create a new service account and link it to a <NT>Bitrise</NT> <NT>Workspace</NT>:

1. <NT>Set up</NT> a <NT>Google Play</NT> service account in <NT>Google Play console</NT>: [Using a service account](https://developers.google.com/android-publisher/getting_started#service-account).
1. Grant the necessary rights to the service account with your [Google Play Console](https://play.google.com/apps/publish). Go to **<NT>Settings</NT>**, then **<NT>Users & permissions</NT>**, then **<NT>Invite new user</NT>**. Due to the way the <NT>Google Play</NT> Publisher API works, you have to grant at least the following permissions to the service account:

   - Access level: View app information.
   - <NT>Release management</NT>: Manage production releases, manage testing track releases.
   - Store presence: Edit store listing, pricing & distribution.
1. Create a JSON key for the service account: [Create a service account key](https://cloud.google.com/iam/docs/keys-create-delete#creating).
1. <Partial_OpeningTheWorkspaceSettingsPage />
1. On the left, select **<NT>Integrations</NT>**.
1. Find and click **<NT>Google Play</NT>**.

   ![google-service-account_png.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-86e5dd1c-f147-0a08-49bb-da4f53b9a960.png)
1. Click **<NT>Add service account</NT>**.
1. Type a name in the **<NT>Name</NT>** field.
1. In the **<NT>Credentials</NT>** section, drag and drop your JSON key file or click **<NT>Upload</NT> a JSON file** to upload it from your computer.

   ![add-json-file.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-48a9ebfc-c07d-81f3-7b87-09f6fdcc1794.png)
1. Once done, click **<NT>Add account</NT>**.

## Connecting a Google service account to a project

To configure a <NT>Bitrise</NT> <NT>project</NT> to use a given <NT>Google Play Console</NT> service account:

1. <Partial_GettingToTheProjectSettingsPage />
1. On the left, select the **<NT>Integrations</NT>** menu option.
1. Go to the **<NT>Stores</NT>** tab.
1. Find the **<NT>Google Play Store</NT>** section.
1. Click the **<NT>Select</NT>...** button.

   ![select-button.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-d9ad5624-810e-4fbf-507a-f900e03620e5.png)
1. In the pop-up window, select an account from the dropdown menu labeled with **<NT>Select an account</NT>**.

   ![attach-service-account.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-f8d70389-5398-fa2c-3a98-db70ffa63853.png)
1. Click **<NT>Save</NT>**.
