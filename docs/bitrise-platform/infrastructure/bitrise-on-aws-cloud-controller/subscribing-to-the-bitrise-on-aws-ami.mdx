The <NT>Bitrise</NT> on <NT>AWS</NT> AMI is now available for both <NT>Linux</NT> and macOS. It enables running <NT>Bitrise</NT> builds on <NT>Amazon EC2</NT> instances.

<NT>Bitrise</NT> on <NT>AWS</NT> is sold via <NT>AWS</NT> Private Offer and a <NT>Bitrise</NT> account is required before you can use <NT>Bitrise</NT>-provided AMIs. Please don't purchase the offerings via the public <NT>AWS</NT> Marketplace.

You can get a private offer of the <NT>Bitrise</NT> on <NT>AWS</NT> listings:

1. After [contacting Bitrise](https://bitrise.io/contact), we will send the order in a pdf format. This document contains all the details of our <NT>Bitrise</NT> on <NT>AWS</NT> offering, including the relevant customer data, the service provided, and the price of the service.
1. Once satisfied with the terms, contact <NT>Bitrise</NT> again to give up permission to create the <NT>AWS</NT> private offer. The offer will be visible on the <NT>AWS</NT> Marketplace.
1. Next you will receive a link to an <NT>AWS</NT> private offer which will also contain the order form attached. The link should take you to the <NT>AWS</NT> Marketplace.

   ![aws-offer-1.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-286c4817-1c22-6e11-06ec-26a34ce81273.png)
1. Optionally, you can enter a purchase order ID for invoicing purposes here: click **<NT>Add</NT> a purchase order**.

   To learn more about purchase orders, check out the official <NT>AWS</NT> documentation: [Managing your purchase orders](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/awsaccountbilling/latest/aboutv2/manage-purchaseorders.html).
1. Review the terms of the agreement. The pdf document will be available under the End User License Agreement link below the **<NT>Create</NT> Contract** button.

   Note that on the bottom right, you might see other available offers, including the <NT>Bitrise</NT> public offer. These are not relevant: the link you received took you straight to your private offer.
1. If everything is in order, click **<NT>Create</NT> Contract** on the right. After this point, the contract can’t be modified on the <NT>AWS</NT> page.

   ![aws-offer-2.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-dd9a98bf-41aa-d933-c06f-34d69bb56148.png)
1. Once the contract is ready, <NT>AWS</NT> should display a message about receiving a license. At this point you can proceed to configure your AMI by clicking **<NT>Continue</NT> to <NT>Configuration</NT>**.

   ![license-mgt.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-594370f7-8b76-f5b1-3042-d09c9f06bfe7.png)
1. Choose a fulfillment option, a version and your region before launching. You can read more about launching software from the <NT>AWS</NT> Marketplace in the official <NT>AWS</NT> documentation: [Launching container software from AWS Marketplace](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/marketplace/latest/buyerguide/buyer-configuring-a-product.html).
1. Once ready, click **<NT>Continue</NT> to Launch** to launch the software.

   ![continue-to-launch.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-d1f6b886-e707-4116-3eb6-331b765507ba.png)

To read more about configuring your <NT>Amazon EC2</NT> instance and running builds there: [Launching an EC2 instance for the Bitrise AMI](/en/bitrise-platform/infrastructure/bitrise-on-aws--manual-setup/launching-an-ec2-instance-for-the-bitrise-ami).
