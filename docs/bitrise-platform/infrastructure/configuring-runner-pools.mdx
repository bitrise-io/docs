You can run <NT>Bitrise</NT> builds on hardware you control. We offer two different options:

- [Running builds on Amazon EC2 instances](/en/bitrise-platform/infrastructure/bitrise-on-aws--manual-setup/advanced-options-for-ec2-instances).
- [Using our on-premise runner](/en/bitrise-platform/infrastructure/running-bitrise-builds-on-premise) to run builds on your own machines, or at other cloud providers.

Both options require setting up runner pools in your <NT>Bitrise</NT> <NT><GlossTerm baseform="workspace">Workspace</GlossTerm></NT>. This reserves build runners for use in your self-hosted builds. Each reserved pool comes with a unique token: this token is required for both an <NT>Amazon EC2</NT> instance and the on-premise runner to be able to connect to your <NT>Bitrise</NT> <NT>Workspace</NT> and run builds of the apps owned by the <NT>Workspace</NT>. You can configure and run these builds from the <NT><GlossTerm baseform="workflow editor">Workflow Editor</GlossTerm></NT>, as any other build.

To do so:

1. <Partial_OpeningTheWorkspaceSettingsPage />
1. On the left, select **<NT>Self-hosted infrastructure</NT>**.
1. Select the **<NT>Bitrise runners</NT>** tab.
1. In the **<NT>Runner pools</NT>** section, click **<NT>Add new</NT>**.

   ![_2025-11-03-runner-pool.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-3ca810bc-4569-859b-f0be-784644322a22.png)
1. Type a name into the **<NT>Name</NT>** field then click **<NT>Next</NT>**.

   ![add-pool.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-e5387f19-bcbb-472b-a2fa-9c9d9aeb1c6d.png)
1. You will receive a token. Copy this token: you will need it for authentication.
1. Open the app you need on <NT>Bitrise</NT> and click **<NT><GlossTerm baseform="workflow">Workflow</GlossTerm></NT>s**.
1. Click **Edit <NT><GlossTerm baseform="bitrise.yml">bitrise.yml</GlossTerm></NT>** to go the <NT>Workflow Editor</NT>, and select **<NT><GlossTerm baseform="stack">Stack</GlossTerm></NT>s & Machines** on the left navigation menu.
1. Find your own stack in the stack selection dropdown menus: it will be called **Self-hosted Runner: <Pool name>**.

   ![stack-select.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-1ec5901d-3577-8321-6e4f-faa9fa366c21.png)

   For example, if you named your pool `my-pool`, the stack will be called **Self-hosted Runner: my-pool**.
1. Click **<NT>Save</NT>** in the top right corner.

Now your builds will run using the reserved runner pools
