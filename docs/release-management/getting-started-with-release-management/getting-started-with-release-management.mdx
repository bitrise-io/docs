<NT>Bitrise</NT> <NT>Release Management</NT> simplifies distributing your iOS and <NT>Android</NT> apps to testers or directly to app stores. <NT>Release Management</NT> works by adding one or more <NT>Release Management</NT> apps to a <NT>Bitrise</NT> <NT>project</NT>. You upload installable artifacts (either IPA or APK/AAB files) to <NT>Release Management</NT> and then you can distribute the files to testers or release them to an online store.

You can go to <NT>Release Management</NT> from the Dashboard or by selecting **<NT>Releases</NT>** from the left navigation sidebar. Both ways takes you to the **<NT>Release Management</NT> overview** screen where you will be able to see your apps in the future.

To start using <NT>Release Management</NT>:

1. Learn about the [key concepts of Release Management](/en/release-management/getting-started-with-release-management/release-management-concepts).
1. Make sure you have at least one [Bitrise project](/en/bitrise-build-cache/getting-started-with-the-build-cache/getting-started-with-the-build-cache#adding-a-new-connection-to-the-build-cache).
1. [Add one or more new app(s)](/en/release-management/getting-started-with-release-management/adding-a-new-app-to-release-management).
1. Check out [build distribution for testing](/en/release-management/build-distribution/distributing-builds-to-testers): you can distribute installable artifacts to testers without involving <NT>Google Play</NT> or the App Store.
1. [Connect the app(s)](/en/release-management/getting-started-with-release-management/connecting-an-app): this requires setting up a connection to <NT>App Store Connect</NT> or <NT>Google Play</NT>.
1. Once you have a connected app, [add a new release](/en/release-management/releases/adding-a-new-release).
1. [Configure your release](/en/release-management/releases/configuring-a-release/configuring-auto-upload).
1. [Go through the stages of the release process](/en/release-management/releases/managing-the-release-process/about-the-release-process) to release the app.
