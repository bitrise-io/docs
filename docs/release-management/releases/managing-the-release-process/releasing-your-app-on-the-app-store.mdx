After your app has gone through all previous stages, it is ready for release. You can release your app to all users at the same time, or configure phased release for the <NT>Apple</NT> Store.

## Configuring phased releases for the <NT>Apple</NT> Store

You can release your app to the <NT>Apple</NT> Store in phases. With a phased release, you release your apps in several different stages, with only a certain percentage of users getting the new version with each stage. Users aren't notified that they are in a staged release. Automating the process means you don't have to manually release the app at each stage to a different segment of users: <NT>Release Management</NT> takes care of that automatically.

:::note[Terminology]

Google calls this feature a staged rollout while <NT>Apple</NT> calls it a phased release.

:::

### Enabling phased releases for the <NT>Apple</NT> Store

If you choose this option, your version update will be released over a seven-day period to a percentage of your users (selected at random by their <NT>Apple ID</NT>) who has automatic updates turned on. Users aren’t notified that they're in a phased release of your app.

You can enable phased release before the App Store review stage or during the release stage after a successful App Store review.

<Tabs>
<TabItem value="before-app-store-review" label="Before App Store review" default>

1. On the **<NT>App Store review</NT>** stage, find **<NT>App Store release settings</NT>**.
1. On the right of the **<NT>Phased release for automatic updates</NT>** card, click **<NT>Change</NT>**.
1. Select the **<NT>Release update over 7-day period using phased release</NT>** option.

   ![phased-release-7-day.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-05ba3ed0-0f1c-c4be-e46f-cc500b21041c.png)
1. Click **<NT>Save changes</NT>**.

</TabItem>
<TabItem value="after-app-store-review" label="After App Store review">

1. On the **<NT>App Store review</NT>** stage or the **Release** stage, find the **<NT>Version release</NT>** card and click **<NT>Change</NT>**.
1. In the dialog, choose one of three options:

   - **<NT>Manually release this version</NT>**
   - **<NT>Automatically release this version</NT>**
   - **<NT>Automatically release this version</NT> after App Review, not earlier than** and set a date.

   ![release-version.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-4f7e400b-3a64-eb42-5b85-c51a3f41da03.png)
1. On the **Release** stage, find the **<NT>Phased release for automatic updates</NT>** card, and click **<NT>Change</NT>**.
1. Select the **<NT>Release update over 7-day period using phased release</NT>** option.

</TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Pausing a phased release

While your app is in phased release, you can choose to pause the release for a total of 30 days. There’s no limit to the number of pauses.

1. On the **Release** stage, click the **<NT>Pause</NT>** button.
1. To continue the phased release, click the **<NT>Continue</NT>** button.

## Releasing your app

Once everything is configured, you can release your app. Depending on your settings, you can either release the app to all your users at the same time, or do a staged rollout.

1. Open your release.
1. Select **Release** on the left navigation bar.
1. Click **<NT>Release version</NT>**. It either releases your app to all users or starts a phased release, depending on your settings. You can change the settings in the **<NT>App Store release settings</NT>** section.

   ![app-store-release.png](/img/_paligo/uuid-fafa8157-e6f0-6495-1da6-7112515830d8.png)

:::note[Changing the date of an automatic release]

For iOS apps, you can change the date on the **Release** page if you selected **<NT>Automatically release this version after App Review, no earlier than</NT>** in the App Store review stage.

:::
