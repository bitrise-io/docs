To perform actions in <NT>Release Management</NT> , your account must have the required access. This access is controlled by permissions. A permission is the ability to perform a specific action, such as adding and connecting apps, creating releases, or changing connected <NT>app settings</NT>.

## Roles and permissions overview

You can assign different roles to different team members. In <NT>Release Management</NT>, there are five different roles on three different levels:

- **<NT>Workspace</NT>-level roles**: These roles aren't tied to a specific <NT>Release Management</NT> app, nor can you change them in <NT>Release Management</NT>. [They are fully tied to the Workspace](/en/bitrise-platform/workspaces/collaboration-and-permissions-in-workspaces/workspace-collaboration) that owns the <NT>Bitrise</NT> <NT>project</NT> that the connected app belongs to. There are two <NT>Workspace</NT>-level roles:

  - **<NT>Workspace</NT> owner**: <NT>Workspace</NT> owners have full administrative control over all aspects of an app in <NT>Release Management</NT>, without any limits or exceptions.
  - **<NT>Workspace</NT> manager**: The main purpose of <NT>workspace</NT> managers is to manage <NT>workspace</NT> collaboration. In <NT>Release Management</NT>, they can add a new app when creating a new project. They, however, can't add a new RM app under an already existing project.
  - **<NT>Contributor</NT>** and **<NT>Viewer</NT>**: These <NT>Workspace</NT>-level roles have no default access to <NT>Release Management</NT> apps.
- **<NT>Project Admin</NT>**: The only [project-level role](/en/bitrise-platform/projects/roles-and-permissions-for-bitrise-ci) in <NT>Release Management</NT>. Adding a user to a project doesn't automatically give them any permissions in <NT>Release Management</NT>, with the exception of users with the Admin role. Project Admins have full administrative rights to apps, builds, <NT>Release Management</NT>, and app deployment with one exception: the Project Admin cannot add a new app with a new project to <NT>Release Management</NT>. When adding a new app to <NT>Release Management</NT>, they can only add it under the project of which they are admin.
- **<NT>Release Management</NT>-level roles**: These are the roles you can configure within <NT>Release Management</NT>.

  - **Release manager**: The main purpose of Release Managers is to handle releases to online stores. They can't add new apps or access <NT>app settings</NT>.
  - **<NT>App</NT> tester**: The App tester role is meant for internal testers. They can access an app and its artifacts, and the [build distribution](urn:resource:component:92118) and [tester groups](/en/release-management/build-distribution/tester-groups) menu. They have no other access.
